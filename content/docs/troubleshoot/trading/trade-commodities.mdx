### Understanding Futures Contracts

<div className="my-4">
  <p className="text-sm text-muted-foreground mb-4">
    Futures contracts are standardized agreements to buy or sell a specific quantity of a commodity at a predetermined price on a future date. They allow you to speculate on price movements without owning the physical asset.
  </p>

  <div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">
    <div className="p-5 rounded-lg border border-green-500/20 bg-gradient-to-br from-green-500/5 to-transparent">
      <div className="font-bold mb-2 flex items-center gap-2 text-green-400">
        <span>‚úì</span>
        <span>Advantages</span>
      </div>
      <ul className="text-sm text-muted-foreground space-y-2">
        <li>‚Ä¢ High liquidity for easy entry/exit</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Leverage to amplify returns</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Ability to profit from falling prices (short selling)</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ No storage or insurance costs</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Tight bid-ask spreads</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Hedging capability against price risks</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div className="p-5 rounded-lg border border-red-500/20 bg-gradient-to-br from-red-500/5 to-transparent">
      <div className="font-bold mb-2 flex items-center gap-2 text-red-400">
        <span>‚ö†</span>
        <span>Risks</span>
      </div>
      <ul className="text-sm text-muted-foreground space-y-2">
        <li>‚Ä¢ Leverage amplifies losses as well as gains</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Contract expiration requires rollover</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Margin calls if positions move against you</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Complexity requires market knowledge</li>
        <li>‚Ä¢ Potential for rapid, significant losses</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<Callout type="warn">
